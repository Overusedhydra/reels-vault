# Scripting & Storytelling

How to structure content so people actually watch until the end.

## Core Principles

- **Open loops** — Start something, finish it later
- **Stakes** — What's at risk? What happens if they don't act?
- **Specificity** — "Made $47K" beats "made a lot of money"
- **Conversational tone** — Write like you talk, not like a textbook
- **One idea per sentence** — Short sentences, easy to follow

## By Creator

### [[Alex Hormozi]] — Hook-Meat-CTA
1. **Hook** — Grab attention (3-5 sec)
2. **Meat** — Educate on offer/problem/solution (30-60 sec)
3. **CTA** — Tell them exactly what to do (5-10 sec)

> "Ads are made up of three parts. Hook, meat, CTA." — Hormozi

**Source:** [[Alex Hormozi — Ad Volume Framework]]

### [[Gary Vaynerchuk]] — Story First
- Lead with the story, not the lesson
- Real moments > scripted content
- "Day in the life" as storytelling vehicle

### [[Ali Abdaal]] — The Transformation Arc
- Before state → struggle → breakthrough → after state
- Specific numbers throughout
- End with "you can do this too"

## Frameworks

### The 3-Part Structure (Hormozi)
1. **Hook** — Grab attention
2. **Meat** — Educate/entertain
3. **CTA** — Tell them what to do next

### Story Arc
1. **Setup** — Context, who, what
2. **Conflict** — Problem, tension, obstacle
3. **Resolution** — How it was solved
4. **Lesson** — What we learned

### AIDA Formula
- **A**ttention — Hook them
- **I**nterest — Keep them watching
- **D**esire — Make them want it
- **A**ction — Tell them what to do

### PAS Formula (Copywriting)
- **P**roblem — Identify the pain
- **A**gitate — Make it worse
- **S**olution — Present the fix

## Meat Formats (Hormozi's 5 Types)

1. **Demonstration** — Show the product/work in action
2. **Testimonial** — Let results speak
3. **Educational** — Teach something valuable
4. **Story** — Narrative-driven content
5. **Faceless** — No person on camera, pure content

## Scripting Checklist

- [ ] Does the hook grab attention in 1 second?
- [ ] Is there an open loop within the first 10 seconds?
- [ ] Are sentences short and conversational?
- [ ] Is there a clear promise of what they'll learn?
- [ ] Does the CTA tell them exactly what to do?
