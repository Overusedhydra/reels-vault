# Hooks & Openings

The first 3 seconds determine if anyone watches the rest. Every creator has their own take on what works.

## Core Principles

- **Pattern interrupt** — Break the scroll with something unexpected
- **Curiosity gap** — Open a loop the viewer needs closed
- **Bold claim** — State something controversial or surprising
- **Visual hook** — Something unusual on screen immediately
- **Direct address** — "You need to hear this" / "Stop scrolling"

## Hook Formulas

### The Controversial Take
```
[Popular belief] is wrong. Here's why.
```
**Example:** "Morning routines are overrated. Here's why."
**Used by:** Hormozi, GaryVee

### The Specific Number
```
[Number] [things/steps/ways] to [desirable outcome]
```
**Example:** "7 ways to double your content output this week"
**Used by:** Hormozi, Ali Abdaal

### The Time Proof
```
I [did X] for [time period]. Here's what happened.
```
**Example:** "I posted 3x/day for 90 days. Here's what happened."
**Used by:** Ali Abdaal, Iman Gadzhi

### The Stop Scrolling
```
Stop scrolling. [Bold claim about what you're about to show].
```
**Example:** "Stop scrolling. This one hack saved me 20 hours last week."
**Used by:** Short-form best practice

## Hooks by Format

### Reels/TikTok/Shorts
- "Wait, so you're telling me..."
- "I tested [thing] for 30 days"
- "Nobody talks about this but..."
- "The algorithm doesn't want you to know..."
- "Stop doing [common thing]"

### YouTube
- "I spent [time/money] to [result]"
- "Why [popular thing] is wrong"
- "The truth about [topic]"
- "[Number] mistakes you're making"

### Ads
- "This [product] changed everything"
- "I was skeptical but..."
- "If you [have this problem], watch this"
